Barycentric Coordinates

As described in GAMES101, barycentric coordinates are useful in graphics for interpolating information from triangle vertices to its surface. A point is written p=αA+βB+γCp=\alpha A+\beta B+\gamma Cp=αA+βB+γC.

Physical Interpretation

If triangle vertices have masses Ma,Mb,McM_a,M_b,M_cMa​,Mb​,Mc​, its center of mass is:

center=MaA+MbB+McCMa+Mb+Mc.\text{center} = \frac{M_aA+M_bB+M_cC}{M_a+M_b+M_c}. center=Ma​+Mb​+Mc​Ma​A+Mb​B+Mc​C​.

The coefficients are nonnegative and sum to 1, exactly the barycentric-coordinate requirements. Thus (α,β,γ)(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)(α,β,γ) represents the proportions of mass assigned to the three vertices. Nonnegative masses put the center inside the object, and the proportions sum to the total mass.

Write the point another way:

p=A+uAB→+vAC→.p=A+u\overrightarrow{AB}+v\overrightarrow{AC}. p=A+uAB+vAC.

For a point inside the triangle, u,v≥0u,v\ge0u,v≥0. Rearranging:

u(B−A)+v(C−A)+A−p=0,u(B-A)+v(C-A)+A-p=0, u(B−A)+v(C−A)+A−p=0,

so:

(1−u−v)A+uB+vC=p.(1-u-v)A+uB+vC=p. (1−u−v)A+uB+vC=p.
